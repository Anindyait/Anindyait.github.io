Advancing Accessibility: ASL Visual Recognition Technology through EfficientNet

Priyanka Mazumdar1, Anindya Biswas1, Aritra Mandal1, Anirban Naskar2 and Soumya Sen1

1. A.K. Choudhury School of Information Technology, University of Calcutta
2. Department of Computer Science and Engineering, University of Calcutta

In short...

In this research, we address the persistent challenge of accurately recognizing American Sign Language (ASL) signs using machine learning, which is crucial for effective communication among Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing individuals. We present a deep learning approach employing EfficientNet, a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture, for real-time ASL sign recognition. Our model is trained on an extensive dataset encompassing a wide range of ASL signs, achieving high validation accuracy. The proposed system has the potential to facilitate seamless and inclusive interactions, bridging the communication gap between Deaf and hearing individuals.
